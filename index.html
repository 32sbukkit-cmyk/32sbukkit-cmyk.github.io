Case Report: Great Lakes Crime Scene

CASE STATUS: ACTIVE / CLASSIFIED

The Suspects

  • Suspect 1: Sewage (Pollution) - Responsible for carrying germs and extra nutrients into the water.
  • Suspect 2: Zebra Mussels (Invasive Species) - Small shellfish that multiply quickly and steal food from local fish.

Evidence of the Crime

  • Sewage makes the water dirty and carries harmful germs.
  • Extra nutrients from sewage can make the water unsafe for humans.
  • Zebra Mussels eat all the plankton that local fish need to survive.
  • Mussels multiply so fast they take over habitats.
  • Native species are being pushed out by these intruders.
  • The overall water quality is at risk if these suspects aren't stopped.

Intervention Rankings

Solution Rank Effectiveness Notes
Water Treatment Plants 8/10 Cleans water well, but old pipes can still leak in heavy rain.
Digital Sensors 7/10 Smart way to track water flow, but pipes are often too small.
Boat Washing 4/10 Stops the spread to new spots, but doesn't fix current infestations.

Investigator's Conclusion

"The lakes are getting cleaner because our waste water technology is improving, meaning we are winning against Sewage. However, Zebra Mussels are still 'at large' because they spread faster than we can control. We still have a lot of work to do."
